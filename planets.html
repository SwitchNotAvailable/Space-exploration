JUPITER

Jupiter is the fifth from the sun and its known as the biggest planet in our galaxy made up of gas called hydrogen. This planet lacks a solid surface like most other planets and has a ring around it and it has a strong magnetic field and is believed to have the mass 2.5 times more of all the other planets

A study shows that Saturn is the only planet less dense than water

VENUS

Venus is a planet 100km away from the sun and it experiences geological activity just like earth and has a thick silicate mantle around an iron core. Amongst all planets venus has the highest temperature of 400 degrees celcius due to the green house gases

URANUS

Uranus is a planet which is purely different from the rest and is believed to be the lightest with the coldest core and gives out the least heat into space and is believed to have about 27 known satelites

NEPTUNE

Neptune is a small planet with a huge mass and more dense and it being more dense means it radiates a lot of heat but not the most because Jupiter does. Neptune has 14 known satelites with Triton being the largest. Neptune is accompanied in its orbit by several minor planets known as Neptune trojans

MECURY

Mercury (0.4 AU (60 million km; 37 million mi) from the Sun) is the closest planet to the Sun and on average, all seven other planets. The smallest planet in the Solar System Mercury has no natural satellites. Besides impact craters, its only known geological features are lobed ridges that were probably produced by a period of contraction early in its history. Mercury's very tenuous atmosphere consists of atoms blasted off its surface by the solar wind. Its relatively large iron core and thin mantle have not yet been adequately explained. Hypotheses include that its outer layers were stripped off by a giant impact, or that it was prevented from fully accreting by the young Sun's energy
